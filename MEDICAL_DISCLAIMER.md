## Mandatory Conditions for Any Use

This project may only be used:

1. Under direct supervision of a licensed medical professional
2. Strictly within a biomedical research or assistive technology research context
3. After review and approval by the relevant ethics committee or IRB
4. With full informed consent of any human subject
5. After independent electrical safety testing of all hardware

## Contraindications

**Do not use** on individuals with:

- Pacemakers or implanted cardiac devices
- Implanted neural stimulators
- Epilepsy or seizure disorders
- Acute neurological conditions (stroke, acute Bell's palsy)
- Active skin diseases or lesions at electrode sites
- Uncontrolled synkinesis or hemifacial spasm
- Pregnancy (without specific medical evaluation)
- Metal implants in the facial region

## Electrical Safety

Functional electrical stimulation (FES) involves applying electrical current to human tissue.
Risks include:
- Skin irritation or burns (improper impedance)
- Involuntary or excessive muscle contraction
- Discomfort or pain
- In rare cases, systemic effects from improper parameters

All stimulation parameters (current, pulse width, frequency, duration) **must be**:
- Defined by a qualified clinician
- Validated through bench testing before any human use
- Individually calibrated per patient

## No Therapeutic Guarantee

This system makes no claims regarding therapeutic efficacy.
No improvement in facial function, symmetry, or nerve recovery is guaranteed.
Published scientific literature on FES-based facial neuroprosthetics shows varied results.
